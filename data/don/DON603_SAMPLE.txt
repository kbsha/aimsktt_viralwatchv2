Bundibugyo virus disease — Democratic Republic of the Congo and Uganda

As of the reporting date, national authorities have recorded 1792 confirmed cases
and 625 deaths across the affected provinces. The outbreak, caused by a species
with no licensed vaccine and no approved treatment, was declared a Public Health
Emergency of International Concern.

Transmission remains concentrated in North Kivu and South Kivu. Active chains have
been reported in Goma, Nyiragongo and Rutshuru health zones, with additional cases
under investigation in Ibanda and Minova. Several healthcare worker infections have
been documented, raising concern about nosocomial transmission.

The situation carries significant cross-border risk. Goma sits directly across the
border from Rubavu/Gisenyi in Rwanda, and confirmed cases have been reported in
neighbouring Uganda. Ongoing insecurity and population displacement in parts of the
affected area continue to complicate contact tracing, and under-reporting is
considered likely in zones with limited testing access.

Response measures include enhanced surveillance at border crossings, expansion of
treatment capacity, and community engagement in the most affected health zones.
